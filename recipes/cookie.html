Chocolate Chip Cookies

Description

Ingredients

  • Sugar : The recipe starts with 1 ½ cups white sugar. 1 ½ cups white sugar
  • Butter: Beat the sugar with two sticks of softened butter. 1 cup butter, softened
  • Eggs: Two eggs lend moisture and act as a binding agent, which means they help hold the dough together. 2 eggs
  • Vanilla: Vanilla extract enhances the overall flavor of the chocolate chocolate chip cookies. 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • Flour: All-purpose flour gives the cookie dough structure. 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • Cocoa powder: They wouldn’t be chocolate chocolate chip cookies without cocoa powder! ⅔ cup cocoa powder
  • Baking soda: Baking soda acts as a leavener, which means it helps the cookies rise. ¾ teaspoon baking soda
  • Salt: A pinch of salt enhances the overall flavor, but it won’t make the cookies taste salty. ¼ teaspoon salt
  • Chocolate chips: You’ll need two cups of semisweet chocolate chips. 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips
  • Walnuts (optional): Walnuts are optional, but they add welcome crunch. ½ cup chopped walnuts (Optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Beat sugar, butter, eggs, and vanilla in a large bowl until light and fluffy.
  3. Combine flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt in another bowl; stir into butter mixture until well blended. Mix in chocolate chips and walnuts. Drop spoonfuls of dough 2 inches apart onto ungreased cookie sheets.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven just until set, 8 to 10 minutes. Cool slightly on the cookie sheets before transferring to wire racks to cool completely.
